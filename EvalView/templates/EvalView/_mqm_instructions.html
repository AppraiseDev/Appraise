• Higlighting errors:
    • Highlight the text fragment where you have identified a translation error with your cursor or finger.
    • Click repeatedly on the highlighted fragment to increase its severity level or to remove the selection. The available severity levels:
    • Severity:
      • Critical: Renders the entire content unfit for purpose or poses the risk for physical, financial, or reputational harm.
      • Major: Seriously affects the understandability, reliability, or usability of the content for its intended purpose or hinders the proper use of the product or service due to a significant loss or change in meaning or because the error appears in a highly visible or important part of the content.
      • Minor: Does not seriously impede the usability, understandability, or reliability of the content for its intended purpose, but has a limited impact on, for example, accuracy, stylistic quality, consistency, fluency, clarity, or general appeal of the content.
    • If something is missing from the text, mark it as an error on the [MISSING] word.
  • Score: Highlighting more errors or changing their severity levels automatically updates the translation quality score on the slider. After highlighting all errors, before continuing to the next screen, please manually update this score to better reflect your judgement. The quality levels associated with numerical scores on the slider:
    • 0: No meaning preserved: Nearly all information is lost between the translation and source. Grammar is irrelevant.
    • 4: Some meaning preserved: The translation preserves some of the meaning of the source but misses significant parts. The narrative is hard to follow due to fundamental errors. Grammar may be poor.
    • 6: Most meaning preserved and few grammar mistakes: The translation retains most of the meaning. It may have some grammar mistakes or minor contextual inconsistencies.
    • 10: Perfect meaning and grammar: The meaning of the translation is completely consistent with the source the context. The grammar is also correct.
