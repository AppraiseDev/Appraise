• Marking spans:
    • Highlight parts of the text with your cursor.
    • Click the span repeatedly to increase its severity.
    • Severity:
      • Critical: Errors that drastically change the meaning or make the content incomprehensible.
      • Major: Errors that cause a change in meaning or a significant reduction in fluency, but do not make the text incomprehensible.
      • Minor: Less severe errors that do not significantly change the meaning or reduce the fluency of the text. They might be small mistakes in grammar, spelling, or punctuation that do not greatly affect the overall quality of the translation.
    • If something is missing from the text, mark it as an error on the [MISSING] word.
  • Score: The translation score is automatically recomputed based on your marked spans. However, in many cases it does reflect well the overall translation quality. Please, update the translation score before continuing to the next segment.
    • 0: No meaning preserved: Nearly all information is lost between the translation and source. Grammar is irrelevant.
    • 4: Some meaning preserved: The translation preserves some of the meaning of the source but misses significant parts. The narrative is hard to follow due to fundamental errors. Grammar may be poor.
    • 6: Most meaning preserved and few grammar mistakes: The translation retains most of the meaning. It may have some grammar mistakes or minor contextual inconsistencies.
    • 10: Perfect meaning and grammar: The meaning of the translation is completely consistent with the source the context. The grammar is also correct.
